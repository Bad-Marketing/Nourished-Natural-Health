Strategic Overview
Strategic Objective

Transition the Amazon catalog to Nourished Natural Health's next generation of clinically formulated products.

Strategic Summary

Q3 will focus on implementing Nourished Natural Health's next generation of clinically formulated products across the Amazon marketplace. Multiple products are receiving formulation upgrades, enhanced scientific positioning, premium packaging and pricing updates.

The priority is to successfully execute these changes across the catalog, clearly communicate the value behind the new formulations and support their adoption through coordinated catalog updates, content, advertising and organic visibility.

Successfully implement the upgraded formulations across the Amazon catalog while ensuring a smooth transition for existing and new customers.

AndroEase Plus Transition

Develop a dedicated transition plan covering:

  • Listing update timeline
  • Inventory transition strategy
    • Create a dedicated SKU for the upgraded formulation to support a controlled inventory transition
    • Coordinate the listing update with incoming inventory to minimize the risk of customers receiving a different formulation than the one represented on the listing
  • Packaging rollout
  • Customer communication throughout the transition
  • Coordination between updated listing content and incoming inventory

Objective: Ensure listing content accurately reflects the product customers receive throughout the transition.

Creative Direction

This quarter's creative direction centers on showing the formulation upgrades in a way that clearly demonstrates the improvements and increases consumer trust in the products' efficacy. Across AndroEase Plus and ProbIron, the products have moved from general-purpose formulas to clinically dosed, standardized, third-party verified ones. The image stack, A+ Content, and Brand Story all need to make that shift immediately visible to a shopper scrolling on mobile.

For AndroEase Plus, since this is a listing update rather than a new launch, the priority is making the upgrade read as a clear step forward without disrupting the trust already built with existing consumers.

We need to implement a staggered approach considering the formula is changing prior to the packaging, to ensure consumers are guided deliberately through the change.

Phase 1 New Formula, Existing Packaging
  • Main Image:
    • Keep packaging the same
    • Edit ingredient collage around bottle - remove White Peony
  • Supplement Facts:
    • Full replace - new doses, copper and reishi standardization added, white peony removed. This is high priority to implement immediately upon formula change.
  • Ingredient benefit breakdown image:
    • White peony removal
    • Substitute with nettle root
  • Old vs New Packaging
    • Remove - this doesn't apply anymore as the formula isn't the same
    • We'll bring a version of this into Phase 2 when packaging changes
  • A+ Content:
    • Remove all white peony references
    • Add video (to be provided by NNH) clearly describing the formulation changes and benefits
  • Brand Story
    • Input 'New Formula' module
  • Storefront:
    • Update comparison chart between NNH v Other Brands to emphasise improved testing
    • AndroEase Plus Store Page:
      • Remove all peony references
      • Update nettle leaf to nettle root
      • Create new formula module highlighting improvements and benefits
Phase 2 Packaging Update
  • To be implemented once inventory is inbound
  • Swap all images to new bottles across the PDP and Store
  • Introduce a version of the old-vs-new comparison graphic, focusing on bottle rather than formula - e.g. "same great formula, upgraded packaging"
  • Keep a "you may receive either packaging" disclaimer live during the bridge overlap window

For ProbIron, we're launching fresh with no review history and a significantly repositioned price point, so the creative needs to do more of the trust-building work up front. The image stack will lead with a premium, elevated look from the very first image, followed quickly by the core differentiator, the clinically studied Lp299v strain and its specific link to iron absorption, and supporting proof points like third-party verification and improved GI tolerance. Video and Brand Story content will build on this same narrative once the core stack direction is confirmed.

Advertising Strategy
Our Approach

In Q3, advertising supports the catalog transition rather than pushing for aggressive growth. AndroEase Plus is being reformulated, ProbIron v2 launches as a new product, and Pure Inositol is on hold pending its format change. Spending aggressively against products in flux wastes budget and risks hard won rankings.

Four priorities:

1
Protect AndroEase Plus through its formulation change
2
Launch ProbIron v2 from a standing start
3
Rank priority keywords through focused Ranking Sprints
4
Recover shoppers who showed interest but didn't buy
1. Launching ProbIron v2

ProbIron v2 launches with no reviews and at a significantly higher price, so conversion will start lower and build over time.

The price increase works in our favour. At the old price, ProbIron was close to unadvertisable at competitive bid levels. The new price roughly halves advertising cost as a share of each sale, which is what makes this product properly fundable on ads.

Sequencing matters more than spend. We won't turn on meaningful budget until the listing is live with the Lp299v story, VINE reviews are landing, and FBA inventory is confirmed. Paid traffic to a review less listing burns budget at a conversion rate we know will be poor. Reviews first, then spend.

2. Protecting AndroEase Plus

AndroEase Plus is a major part of Amazon revenue and is being reformulated on the same listing. It's the quarter's highest risk item.

The main risk isn't advertising. It's the Buy Box. In 2025, pricing changes dropped Buy Box win rate. When the Buy Box is lost, ads stop showing and rankings decay.

  • Hold spend steady through the transition. Cutting back during a listing change is how rankings get permanently lost.
  • Expect a short-term conversion dip as content changes over, and don't over-react.
  • Add a defensive layer so competitors can't capitalise while the listing is in flux.
  • Pause immediately if the Buy Box is lost.
  • Hold the ranking push until new packaging images are live, so shoppers aren't seeing one product and receiving another.
3. Ranking Sprints

Sprints continue in a more structured format: one product, one keyword set, one tracked outcome, reviewed every two weeks. Each is logged with target keywords, starting rank, budget, target rank and result, so we can decide what to target next based on evidence.

How a sprint works: target keywords with real room to move, push hard on visibility for a defined window, accept a higher cost during that push, then ease back once the product holds position.

4. Inventory Is the Biggest Constraint

When FBA runs out and FBM takes over, conversion collapses. And every stockout resets organic ranking, so we then pay premium bids to buy back rankings we already owned. Stockouts are the most expensive thing happening in this account, and they're entirely preventable.

5. Post Prime Day Recovery

Prime Day delivered $7k+ across four days, within our efficiency target. We'll retarget shoppers who viewed but didn't buy and use Brand Tailored Promotions to bring back high intent visitors. Prime Exclusive Discounts clearly outperformed standard sale prices on visibility, which we'll carry into Q4.
